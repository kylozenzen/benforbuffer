A Community Playbook built for Buffer.

Strategic, but human. Structured, but flexible. Here’s how I’d approach Discord, Reddit, and social engagement as your Senior Community Manager.

Discord

The always-on Buffer lounge

Daily rituals and channels designed around creators and small businesses — not just feature announcements.

  • • Welcome flow that tags creators vs. small biz vs. social pros
  • • Weekly rituals: Wins Monday, Build-in-Public Wednesday, Lab Friday
  • • Private channels for power users & beta testers
  • • Moderation that feels human, not heavy-handed

Reddit

A home base for honest creator talk

Launching a subreddit that centers real workflows, experiments, and unpolished conversations.

  • • Weekly “Show us your week in Buffer” threads
  • • AMAs with creators, small biz owners, and Bufferoos
  • • Template and workflow breakdowns pinned as resources
  • • Clear rules to keep it useful, not salesy

Social Engagement

Showing up where Buffer people already are

Comment-first, human-first engagement across LinkedIn, Threads, Bluesky, and beyond.

  • • Daily pass through key hashtags & creator posts
  • • “We see you” replies that spotlight community wins

  • • Partnering with Advocacy to close the loop

Power Users

Structured beta and power user programs that feel like collaboration, not homework.

  • • Lightweight feedback loops tied to roadmap moments

Signature community programs I’d pilot.

Community thrives on repeatable, human rituals. Here are a few I’d test and iterate with the team.

1. Creator Spotlight Sessions

Monthly interviews or live sessions with Buffer users walking through their systems.

  • • Live in Discord, replayed across socials.
  • • Focus on workflows, not vanity metrics.
  • • Pull quotable moments into Buffer’s marketing content.

2. Buffer Lab Weeks

A themed week where the community experiments with one workflow together.

  • • Themes like “Repurpose Week” or “30-Minute Content Systems”.
  • • Simple daily prompts in Discord & Reddit.
  • • Summary threads that become public resources.

3. Power User Circles

Small groups of power users matched by stage, platform, or niche.

  • • Quarterly cohorts (e.g., Agencies, Solo Creators, Small Biz).
  • • Feedback routes directly into Product and Marketing.
  • • Shared wins surfaced across Buffer’s content.

4. Creator Census

A quarterly “state of the Buffer creator” survey turned into a public, transparent report.

  • • Informs product decisions and content strategy.
  • • Gives community a sense of belonging and visibility.
  • • Aligns with Buffer’s transparency DNA.

How I’d approach my first 90 days at Buffer.

Community isn’t a growth hack — it’s a relationship. Here’s how I’d balance listening, quick wins, and durable systems.

Days 0–30

Listen, map, and earn trust

  • • Deep dive into existing Discord, socials, and any prior community experiments.
  • • 1:1s with Marketing, Advocacy, Product, and Engineering partners.
  • • Community listening sessions: “What do you wish Buffer did more of?”
  • • Identify quick wins that reduce friction for creators and small businesses.

Days 31–60

Launch rituals & pilot programs

  • • Introduce core Discord rituals and refine structure.
  • • Soft-launch the Buffer subreddit with a clear content and engagement plan.
  • • Pilot one flagship program (e.g. Creator Spotlight Sessions).
  • • Formalize how insights flow into Product and Content roadmaps.

Days 61–90

Scale what works, sunset what doesn’t

  • • Expand successful programs and clearly communicate their purpose.
  • • Stand up a structured power user / beta program.
  • • Partner with Growth & Brand to tie community stories into launch moments.
  • • Publish a light, transparent “state of the community” snapshot.
